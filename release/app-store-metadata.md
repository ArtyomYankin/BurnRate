## Description (max 4000 chars per locale)

### EN

```
You have a garage, a hoodie, and an idea worth a billion dollars.

BurnRate is an idle game about scaling an AI startup from a single
engineer in a garage to a galactic-scale model that quietly runs every
dishwasher on the planet. It's a satire — fond, occasionally bleak — of
the world that built it.

— GAMEPLAY —

• Build the pipeline. Engineers, GPUs, Data, and Energy each feed into
  a bottleneck-limited token flow. Balance four chains; ignore one and
  everything stalls.

• Allocate. Every token you earn splits across four departments:
  PRODUCT, R&D, MARKETING, SAFETY. Crank PRODUCT for capital. Crank R&D
  for sprint upgrades. Crank MARKETING to discount the next round.
  Neglect SAFETY and Alignment Debt accrues — and it persists across
  prestige.

• Close funding rounds. Ten of them: Garage, Bootstrap, Coworking,
  Startup Office, Megacorp, Big Tech, Campus, Datacenter, Planetary,
  AGI Singularity. Each prestige resets your producers but earns Equity
  you spend on a permanent Research Tree.

• The model talks back. 70+ vignettes deliver the narrative spine
  through team DMs, leaked emails, board memos, and a fictional tech
  outlet called TechBeat. Some have reply choices — your pick locks in
  and modifies your stats for an hour.

• Training Run gacha. Spend tokens for a probabilistic Tokens
  multiplier. Five tiers from Failed to Breakthrough. First roll is
  free, guaranteed Solid.

— PILLARS —

• Idle-friendly. The game runs while you're away — up to 4 hours of
  offline catchup early game, 8 hours late game.

• Pixel art with a soft cream palette. Designed to look right both at a
  glance and on the third hour of a long session.

• No accounts, no analytics, no ads, no IAP. The whole thing is here.

— LOCALIZATION —

English, Deutsch, Polski.

— FROM THE GDD —

"You will be on the Lex Fridman podcast within 6 weeks."
"The dishwasher on floor 4 has been autonomous for years. It is also
the CEO."

— PRIVACY —

We don't collect data. Saves are local. See the Privacy Policy URL.
```
(~2630 chars — well under 4000)

## Age Rating answers (App Store Connect → Age Rating)

All "None" / "No":
- Cartoon or Fantasy Violence — None
- Realistic Violence — None
- Sexual Content or Nudity — None
- Profanity or Crude Humor — **Infrequent/Mild** (one or two "shit happens" tier lines in vignettes, no f-bombs)
- Alcohol, Tobacco, or Drug Use — **Infrequent/Mild** ("let's get drinks" reply in one vignette)
- Mature/Suggestive Themes — None
- Horror/Fear Themes — **Infrequent/Mild** (cosmic dread vignettes near AGI Singularity)
- Medical/Treatment Information — No
- Gambling (real money) — No
- Contests — No
- Unrestricted Web Access — No
- User-Generated Content — No

**Expected rating: 9+ (Infrequent/Mild Horror).**

If you want 4+ rating, soften the Horror references in the AGI ending
vignettes — but 9+ is fine for idle games and reaches the same audience.

## App Privacy Details (Nutrition Labels)

In App Store Connect → App Privacy:

- **Do you or your third-party partners collect data from this app? → No**

That's the only question. We collect nothing.

(Expo EAS Update hits `u.expo.dev` for JS bundles — Apple does not
classify infrastructure CDN requests as "data collection". If a reviewer
asks: it's the OTA-update mechanism, no user data involved.)

**Beta App Review Notes**:
```
The app is a fully-local idle game. No login, no IAP, no ads, no
analytics. Game saves are local SQLite. Notifications are local
re-engagement reminders only.

The OTA-update mechanism uses Expo EAS Update (u.expo.dev) to deliver
bug fixes — this is the same infrastructure used by hundreds of apps
on the App Store and does not transmit user data.

The hidden dev panel (accessed by tapping the "BURN·RATE" logo 7 times
in 3 seconds) is intentional — it lets us debug live builds without
shipping a debug menu to players.
```
